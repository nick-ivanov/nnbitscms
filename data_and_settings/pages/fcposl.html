Fair Credit Permissive Open Source License

Permissive software licenses, such as BSD, Apache and MIT, contrary to Copyleft, allow companies and individuals to use open source code as part of proprietary software. However, most proprietary developers bury the original copyright in a very boring pile of formal notices and disclaimers, while leaving their own copyright prominent, visible and accessible. The idea of FCPOSL is to give proprietary developers all freedoms they need, but oblige them to credit the original author(s) every time and in the same manner they credit themselves. The following license is a fork of BSD 2-clause (Simplified) license with an additional requirement added.

How to use FCPOSL

  • Create your copy of FCPOSL in a text editor by replacing <YEAR>, <OWNER> <PROGRAM DESCRIPTION>, <NAME>, <OWNER>, <EMAIL>, and <URL> with their values.
  • Feel free changing the credit line with whatever you want. It is your code, and if it is good enough, many companies will make money using it. Don't hesitate making them pay this really small price.
  • Add the text of your license as a comment at the beginning of each source file of your project.
  • Provide a copy of your license as LICENSE.txt in the root directory of your project.
  • Wherever it is needed and possible, make the license available to the users of your software (e.g. pre-installation license agreement, --version/-v CLI options, Help-About menu, etc.)
